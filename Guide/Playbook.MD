# Playbook

## Guiding principles
- Keep a steady economic pipeline running: resource fields, storage capacity, task rewards, and the construction queue must all work together without overflow.
- Before collecting rewards or using hero inventory, spend resources on upgrades with a fast ROI or on prerequisites for your next expansion.
- Use your hero as leverage: safe adventures, points and items consistent with your economy, farming or defense, and always monitor health.
- Protect your growth with crannies, diplomacy, report reading, and crop management: a loss avoided is often worth more than an early upgrade.
- Expand ahead of time: culture points, expansion slots, settlers/chiefs, and capital choice must all be planned before the resources are ready.
- Troops must have a purpose: profitable farming, real defense, an operational hammer, conquest, or alliance support. Avoid unnecessary losses.
- Play to your tribe: adapt your build order, troops, hero, and aggression tempo to its natural bonuses.
- Create value within your alliance: answer messages, share useful intel, coordinate defenses/attacks, and maintain a reliable reputation.
- For artifacts, building plans, and the World Wonder, prepare your logistics and relationships well before release.

## Early game operations
- Complete tasks, raise production, balance resources, and keep construction slots busy whenever the ROI justifies it.
- Don't collect large rewards if your warehouse or granary risks overflowing; start an upgrade or increase capacity first.
- Bring low resource fields up to efficient levels before investing too much in non-essential buildings.
- Check reports and messages every session to spot farming opportunities, social openings, or risks.
- Treat crop and free crop as a constraint: if free crop drops too low, both military and building growth stall.

## Mid game
- Define your account's role: defensive, offensive, hybrid, economic support, or endgame.
- Plan your second and third villages around croppers, oases, distance, safety, and alliance influence.
- Develop a capital that fits the plan: a cropper for military power/endgame, a non-cropper only if your strategy calls for it.
- Use the marketplace, trade routes, and the NPC merchant to cut down on dead time, not to paper over poor planning.

## Military
- Before attacking, weigh profit, distance, the risk of reinforcements, the wall, traps, the cranny, and your return timing.
- Catapults and rams are strategic tools: targets, fakes, and waves must all have a measurable objective.
- A smithy is worth it when troop volume and length of use repay the cost; otherwise, more troops may give a better return.
- Strong defense means information: reports, attack warnings, sniping, your alliance, and reading enemy patterns.

## Endgame
- Artifacts and construction plans require coordination, scouting, server timing, and resource/troop logistics.
- The World Wonder and victory points are network games: supplies, defense, sitters, leadership, and resilience matter more than any single action.
